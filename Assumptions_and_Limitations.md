# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

This project is based on the following assumptions:

1. **The food usage log is intended to support waste tracking and accountability.**  
   The analysis assumes that the log is used (or presented) as a mechanism for understanding food usage and waste over time.

2. **Recorded values are entered in good faith by staff.**  
   This project does not assume intentional misreporting or negligence by individual cooks, runners, or supervisors. The focus is on system design rather than human error.

3. **The structure of the log reflects the organization’s current measurement approach.**  
   The analysis treats the log as representative of how food usage is formally documented, rather than as an informal or temporary tool.

4. **Leadership decisions rely, at least partially, on documented metrics.**  
   The project assumes that logs such as this are meant to inform operational review, reporting, or oversight rather than existing purely for archival purposes.

---

## Limitations

This project has several intentional limitations:

1. **No quantitative waste calculation is performed.**  
   The goal of this analysis is not to estimate actual food waste volumes, but to evaluate whether the measurement system can support such calculations at all.

2. **No access to proprietary datasets or internal systems.**  
   The analysis relies solely on a de-identified example of the food usage log and does not incorporate internal databases, financial records, or patient-level data.

3. **No evaluation of staff performance or behavior.**  
   This project does not assess individual compliance, training, or execution. The findings relate exclusively to metric design and system capability.

4. **Findings may not generalize to all institutional kitchens.**  
   While the issues identified are common in large organizations, this analysis is focused on the specific structure of the examined log and should not be assumed to apply universally.

5. **The proposed improvements are minimal by design.**  
   Suggested changes prioritize feasibility and clarity over comprehensive system redesign. The project does not attempt to prescribe enterprise-scale solutions.

---

## Scope Boundary

This project evaluates **measurement validity**, not operational outcomes.

Any conclusions drawn relate to the **ability of the log to support accountability**, not to the effectiveness, intentions, or competence of the organization or its staff.
